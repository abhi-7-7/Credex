# Go-To-Market Strategy

## The Core Thesis

Credex sells discounted AI credits. The audit tool is the top-of-funnel mechanism: it creates genuine value (showing real savings), captures a qualified lead (someone who cares about AI spend), and introduces Credex as the next step — only when savings are significant enough ($500+/month) to make the conversation credible.

---

## Target Customer

**Primary:** Technical founders and engineering leads at seed-to-Series A startups (5–50 employees) actively using multiple AI tools.

**Qualifying signals:**
- Using 2+ AI dev tools (Cursor + Copilot, or subscriptions + API)
- Monthly AI spend > $200 (enough to care about savings)
- Not yet at the scale where procurement/finance are involved

**Anti-targets:**
- Solo developers on free plans (no spend to audit)
- Enterprise (complex procurement, not self-serve)
- Non-technical decision-makers (won't trust the audit without engineering validation)

---

## Channels

### Channel 1 — Organic / Hacker News (Week 1–2)
**Tactic:** Submit to HN Show HN with transparent methodology. The "finance-person defensible" angle resonates with the HN audience because it's explicitly anti-bullshit.

**Why it works:** Technical founders on HN are the exact ICP. A genuinely useful free tool gets traction without ad spend.

**KPI:** 500 unique visitors in first 48 hours, 50+ audits completed.

### Channel 2 — Twitter/X seeding (Ongoing)
**Tactic:** Shareable result URLs are the distribution mechanism. When someone finds $1,200/month in savings, they share it. The OG preview shows the savings number — that's the hook.

**Target accounts:** Founders/CTOs with 5k–50k followers talking about AI tools. DM with personalised audit using their known stack (e.g. "I audited a typical GPT-4o + Cursor Business setup — here's what I found").

**KPI:** 10 shares generating >100 visits each within first month.

### Channel 3 — Community drops (Week 2–4)
Relevant communities: Indie Hackers, r/startups, YC alumni Slack (via members), Lenny's community. Drop as a resource post, not an ad.

**Framing:** "Built a free tool to audit AI spend — happy to get feedback" (honest, high trust).

### Channel 4 — SEO long-tail (Month 2+)
Target: "how much does cursor cost for teams", "github copilot vs copilot business", "openai api batch pricing". These are bottom-of-funnel searches from people already evaluating costs.

---

## Conversion Flow

```
Audit completed → Results shown → Email captured → Credex intro email (day 3)
                                                  → Follow-up if no reply (day 7)
```

**CTA logic:**
- Savings < $500/month: show results + email capture. No Credex push (low intent).
- Savings ≥ $500/month: show Credex CTA prominently + email capture. High intent.

**Email sequence:**
1. Day 0: Audit report (sent immediately on capture)
2. Day 3: "Did you act on the recommendations?" + Credex intro
3. Day 7: "Here's what one customer saved with Credex credits" (social proof)

## First 100 Customers

1. Manually audit 20 startups using public info (known stacks from job postings, GitHub orgs). Share personalised reports on Twitter tagging the founders.
2. DM 50 YC founders from public batch lists with a personalised note referencing their specific stack.
3. Post in 3 Slack communities. Not a product launch post — a resource post offering the audit as a service.
4. Write one case study post: "We audited 50 startup AI stacks — here's what we found." Publish on Substack and cross-post to HN.

---

## Competitive Positioning

**vs. generic expense management tools:** Those tools don't know AI pricing nuances (model tiers, batch API, prompt caching). We do.

**vs. doing it manually:** Takes 3–4 hours with spreadsheets. We do it in 60 seconds with verifiable logic.

**vs. asking the AI vendors themselves:** They have no incentive to recommend cheaper alternatives. We do.
